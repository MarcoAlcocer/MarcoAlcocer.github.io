Welcome!

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Political Science at the University of California, San Diego. My research lies at the intersection between organized crime and politics. I am primarily interested in understanding (1) how government policies affect organized crime and their behavior, including their illicit activities, territorial presence, and use of violence, and (2) how criminal organizations attempt, oftentimes successfully, to influence public policy by capturing government institutions.

My book-style dissertation investigates how the Mexican government’s crackdown of 2007 created incentives for organized crime to diversify their activities and expand their presence to territories where they had previously not operated. It then analyzes how criminal organizations have attempted, often successfully, to capture the public security institutions and local governments in these new territories.

Prior to my PhD, I worked on drug policy at ONDCP, with refugees and immigrants at USCRI, and with unaccompanied immigrant minors in detention centers at the Young Center for Immigrant Children's Rights.

I have a M.A. in International Security from the Schar School of Policy and Government and Bachelor Degrees in Political Science and Spanish from Southwestern University.

I am a proud Mexican immigrant, love playing and watching fútbol, and passionate about helping the communities that I am a part of.
